Coach of Zambia Women’s World Cup team accused of sexual misconduct, report claims<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The coach of the Zambia team preparing to make its debut at the Women\u2019s World Cup has been accused of sexual misconduct and the case was referred to world body FIFA last year for investigation, according to\u00a0a report in The Guardian newspaper.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bruce Mwape was accused of coercing players into having sex with him if they wanted to keep their place on the team, according to the report published by the British newspaper on Saturday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Other Zambian coaches and officials were also accused of sexual misconduct and investigated, including the Under-17 girls\u2019 team coach, according to the paper.<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Christine Sinclair will lead the Canadian women\u2019s national team at the upcoming Women\u2019s World Cup. Sinclair, international soccer\u2019s all-time leading scorer among men and women, is playing in her sixth World Cup.<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Rising star Trinity Rodman scored for U.S. in the 76th minute and again in the 88th, and the Americans finally capitalized on their many offensive chances to beat Wales 2-0 in a final tuneup for the Women\u2019s World Cup.<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Cameron Smith has his first victory of the year. The Australian shot a 68 on Sunday to win LIV Golf-London by one stroke over Patrick Reed and Marc Leishman<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mirra Andreeva didn\u2019t go far when she needed some advice after losing in the third round at her first major tennis tournament.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Zambia soccer association president Andrew Kamanga confirmed in a message to The Associated Press on Sunday that allegations of sexual misconduct were referred to FIFA and Zambian police last year and said it was \u201can old story.\u201d Kamanga didn\u2019t name any of the people facing allegations of wrongdoing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In line with protocol, FIFA said its independent ethics committee would not comment on whether or not there was an investigation underway.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Zambia\u2019s soccer association launched\u00a0its own investigation\u00a0last year but also didn\u2019t name any officials, coaches or players involved at the time. The Zambian association said when it made the announcement in September that it was taking the allegations seriously.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But The Guardian quoted an anonymous source as saying that the Zambian association was \u201cturning a blind eye\u201d to the allegations because of the recent success of its women\u2019s team under Mwape.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Zambia qualified for the Tokyo Olympics in 2021 and the World Cup in Australia and New Zealand, which starts this month. The men\u2019s team has never made it to the World Cup.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Zambia on Friday had\u00a0a surprising 3-2 win over two-time World Cup winner Germany\u00a0in a warmup game for this year\u2019s tournament. Zambia captain and star striker Barbra Banda scored twice, including the winning goal in the 12th minute of added time.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Banda has been at the center of another controversy for the Zambian soccer association after she was withdrawn from last year\u2019s Women\u2019s African Cup of Nations because of\u00a0a bungled sex eligibility case.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Zambia\u2019s football association removed Banda from its squad for the tournament over sex eligibility rules despite Banda being cleared to play at the Olympics and at the World Cup. The association and the African soccer confederation blamed each other for mishandling Banda\u2019s case.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Zambia is in Group C at the World Cup with Spain, Costa Rica and Japan and plays its first game against the Japanese on July 22.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mwape was featured in\u00a0an interview on the FIFA website\u00a0in May, when he spoke about taking Zambia to the World Cup for the first time.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
